There are moments when you realise that the dream you kept putting off — "maybe next time", "maybe when I'm more ready" — has already come knocking. Joining the mărțișoare market at Esplanada Oancea was one of those moments. With over 600 hand-crafted spring charms, tired fingers, and a heart as full as the stand, we lived four days I will never forget.

*A quick note for our international friends: a **mărțișor** (plural: mărțișoare) is a small handmade charm traditionally gifted in Romania on the 1st of March to mark the arrival of spring. It's worn close to the heart — a tiny, beautiful ritual.*

## Day 1 – We did it!

The morning started chaotically, as all adventures worth telling do. Boxes to carry, shelves to assemble, tags to pin — and nerves that had absolutely no intention of settling down. But when the last charm found its place on the shelf and the first person stopped to look at the stand, something shifted in me.

People smiled. They touched each piece with care, as if they somehow knew that behind it lay a story. Sales on the first day far exceeded our expectations. We drove home with tired legs, but with a strange, warm energy in our chests — the kind of beautiful exhaustion you give yourself on purpose, with love.

## Day 2 – A full heart despite the cold

<Image src={ziua2} alt="The Gifted Piggy at the stand on day 2 – cold weather, colours, and a smile" width={700} height={933} class="rounded-xl my-6 mx-auto" />

The second day brought wind and a sky that couldn't decide between clouds and light. The temperatures weren't exactly inviting for a stroll, but people came anyway. Some hurried past, others stopped, looked, touched — and a few came back after they had already walked away, pulled back by something they couldn't leave behind.

I dressed in every colour I owned, trying to make up for the cold with joy. A woman bought six charms at once — one for every woman in her family. She chose with a care you rarely see at a market, asking about each design, as if she needed to be sure each person received exactly the right one. Moments like these remind you why you do what you do.

## Day 3 – The sun came out!

<Image src={ziua3} alt="The Gifted Piggy stand on day 3 – sunshine and colourful products" width={700} height={933} class="rounded-xl my-6 mx-auto" />

Day three arrived with sunshine and a crowd to match. The esplanade was alive — music in the background, children weaving between stalls, adults pausing to admire. And there we were, with our colourful stand and charms glinting in the March light.

It was the day I felt most strongly that we were part of something bigger than ourselves — that old, beautiful ritual of spring, of the small gift worn close to the heart. Stitch watched from the shelf with his enormous eyes, and people laughed when they spotted him. After all, our products always carry a little piece of our personality.

## Day 4 – The last day, and a heartfelt tally

<Image src={ziua4} alt="The Gifted Piggy stand on day 4 – products arranged before opening" width={700} height={933} class="rounded-xl my-6 mx-auto" />

The last day of a market always has a particular flavour. Bittersweet — you know it's ending, yet you want to hold on to every minute. We arranged the stand with even more care than on the first days, and smiled at every customer as if they were the first of the week.

Some people came back who had seen us earlier in the week, this time bringing a friend, a partner, a mother — to show them. *"See, I told you it was worth it."* That sentence is worth all the effort combined.

<Image src={ziua42} alt="The Gifted Piggy stand on day 4 – sunlight on the products" width={700} height={933} class="rounded-xl my-6 mx-auto" />

At the end of the day, as we packed everything up and looked at the nearly empty shelves, I felt a quiet, deep joy. The way things go well when you pour your soul into them — and when you're surrounded by people who believe in you.

## Epilogue – Over 600 stories worn close to the heart

We left Esplanada Oancea with less than a third of what we'd brought. And with far more than we had hoped to gain: real connections, honest feedback, and the confirmation that what we make matters to people. That every hand-crafted charm finds its way to a chest that deserves it.

Thank you to my husband, who carried, assembled, dismantled, and stood by the stand for hours without a single complaint. Thank you to Arya and Diana, who brought energy and help throughout the preparations. And thank you to everyone who stopped at our stand — whether you bought something or not. Your presence was the most beautiful gift of all.

See you at the next market. 🐷🌸
